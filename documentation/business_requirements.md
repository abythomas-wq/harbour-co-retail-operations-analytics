# SwiftFulfil Analytics Platform

## Project Overview

SwiftFulfil Ltd is a fictional UK-based e-commerce fulfilment company that sells consumer products across the United Kingdom and Europe. The company operates multiple warehouses and works with several suppliers and courier partners to fulfil customer orders efficiently.

The objective of this project is to design a relational database, generate realistic operational data, analyse business performance using SQL, and create executive dashboards in Excel.

---

## Business Objectives

The business aims to:

- Improve warehouse efficiency.
- Reduce delivery delays.
- Optimise inventory levels.
- Monitor supplier performance.
- Increase customer satisfaction.
- Support operational decision-making using data.

---

## Stakeholders

- Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Operations Director
- Warehouse Managers
- Procurement Manager
- Logistics Manager
- Customer Service Manager

## Business Processes

The business follows the following operational workflow:

Customer Registration

↓

Customer Places Order

↓

Inventory Allocation

↓

Warehouse Picking

↓

Packing

↓

Courier Dispatch

↓

Delivery

↓

Possible Return

---

## Business Questions

The database should answer questions such as:

- Which warehouse processes the most orders?
- Which products generate the highest revenue?
- Which suppliers deliver late most often?
- What is the average fulfilment time?
- Which products require replenishment?
- What percentage of deliveries are late?
- Which customers generate the highest lifetime value?

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Revenue
- Gross Profit
- Average Order Value
- Order Fulfilment Time
- On-Time Delivery Rate
- Inventory Turnover
- Stockout Rate
- Return Rate
- Supplier On-Time Delivery Rate
- Orders Processed per Employee

## Project Scope

This project includes:

- SQL schema creation
- Synthetic data generation
- SQL analysis
- Excel dashboard development
- Project documentation

---

## Assumptions

- The company operates five warehouses.
- Customers are located across the UK and Europe.
- Products are sourced from multiple suppliers.
- Inventory is managed separately for each warehouse.
- Orders may contain multiple products.
- Some orders may be returned.
