1. Executive Summary
Harbour & Co. Retail Operations Analytics is an end-to-end Business Analysis and data analytics project developed to support Project Horizon — Harbour & Co.'s Omnichannel Transformation Programme.
The project demonstrates how operational data can be transformed into actionable insights to support strategic decision-making across retail operations, supply chain, customer experience, and commercial performance.
The solution establishes a relational data foundation covering:
•	Customer behaviour
•	Sales performance
•	Product performance
•	Inventory operations
•	Supplier management
•	Procurement
•	Order fulfilment
•	Shipment performance
The objective is to provide leadership with improved visibility into operational performance and identify opportunities to improve efficiency, customer experience, and sustainable growth.

4. Project Horizon — Business Challenge
Harbour & Co. initiated Project Horizon, an Omnichannel Transformation Programme designed to strengthen its competitive position within an increasingly challenging retail environment.
As customer expectations continue to evolve towards faster fulfilment, personalised experiences, and seamless movement between digital and physical channels, Harbour & Co. required stronger analytical capabilities to understand:
•	Where commercial value is being created 
•	Which customers and products drive profitability 
•	Whether operational capabilities can support continued digital growth

The primary challenge was not simply increasing sales, but ensuring that growth remained profitable, scalable, and operationally sustainable.
This analytics initiative was developed to provide leadership with a data-driven view of business performance and support strategic decision-making across commercial operations, customer experience, and supply chain functions.

8. Executive Strategic Assessment
The analysis indicates that Harbour & Co. has a strong commercial foundation, with profitable omnichannel operations and clear areas of customer and product value concentration.
However, as the organisation continues its digital transformation journey, operational reliability has emerged as the key constraint to sustainable growth.
The findings from Project Horizon can be summarised across four strategic themes:
1. Omnichannel Growth Remains a Strategic Advantage
Harbour & Co.'s digital channels represent the largest source of commercial value.
Within the analysed dataset:
•	Website, Click & Collect, and Marketplace contribute approximately 70% of revenue and profit. 
•	Channel profitability remains consistent across digital and physical channels. 
This indicates that digital expansion is currently creating profitable growth rather than margin dilution.
Business Implication
The strategic priority should not be shifting investment away from digital channels but ensuring that operational capability scales alongside customer demand.
2. Customer Value is Concentrated Among Existing Relationships
The analysis demonstrates that Harbour & Co.'s strongest commercial opportunity lies within existing customer relationships.
Key findings:
•	Regular customers contribute approximately 55% of revenue. 
•	Loyalty members generate approximately 83% of dashboard revenue. 
•	Silver loyalty members represent the largest loyalty segment. 
Business Implication
Improving retention, personalisation, and loyalty progression is likely to provide stronger long-term value than relying solely on customer acquisition.
3. Product Performance is Concentrated in Core Categories
A small number of categories contribute most of the commercial performance.
Key findings:
•	Lifestyle and Fashion generate approximately 70% of profit. 
•	These categories represent the greatest opportunity and risk due to their commercial importance. 
Business Implication
Harbour & Co. should prioritise:
•	Product availability 
•	Inventory visibility 
•	Assortment quality 
•	Fulfilment performance 
within these strategically important categories.
4. Fulfilment Reliability is the Primary Operational Risk
While supplier lead times are stable, customer delivery performance requires improvement.
Key findings:
•	Approximately 70% of online orders are delivered on time. 
•	Around one in three online orders experiences delay. 
•	Website represents the largest operational exposure due to its scale. 
Business Implication
The immediate operational priority should be improving fulfilment reliability to protect customer experience and enable continued omnichannel growth.
9. Strategic Recommendations
Based on the findings from Project Horizon, the following recommendations are proposed:
Priority 1 — Improve Omnichannel Fulfilment Reliability
Recommendation
Launch a targeted fulfilment improvement programme focused on reducing online delivery delays.

Actions:
•	Establish an on-time delivery target and performance baseline. 
•	Analyse delayed orders by: 
o	Warehouse 
o	Carrier 
o	Fulfilment route 
o	Stock availability 
o	Order ageing 
•	Introduce regular operational performance reviews. 
Expected Impact:
Improved customer experience, reduced delivery failures, and stronger confidence in digital growth.
Priority 2 — Protect High-Value Customer Segments
Recommendation
Develop targeted loyalty and retention strategies focused on valuable customer groups.
Actions:
•	Increase engagement with Regular customers. 
•	Encourage loyalty tier progression. 
•	Introduce personalised offers based on customer behaviour. 
•	Monitor customer lifetime value and repeat purchase behaviour. 
Expected Impact:
Higher retention, increased customer value, and stronger competitive differentiation.
Priority 3 — Protect Core Product Categories
Recommendation
Prioritise investment in commercially significant categories.
Actions:
•	Maintain strong availability for Lifestyle and Fashion categories. 
•	Monitor category-level stock performance. 
•	Review lower-performing subcategories based on: 
o	Profitability 
o	Stock turnover 
o	Strategic relevance 
Expected Impact:
Improved profitability protection and more efficient assortment decisions.
Priority 4 — Strengthen Operational Data Visibility
Recommendation
Expand analytics capability to enable deeper operational diagnosis.
Additional data requirements:
•	Warehouse processing performance 
•	Carrier service-level data 
•	Supplier OTIF performance 
•	Inventory availability at order placement 
•	Stockouts and backorders 
•	Customer returns and refunds 
•	Delivery trends over time 
Expected Impact:
Move from identifying operational problems to predicting and preventing them.
10. Data Limitations & Future Enhancements
The current analysis provides strong visibility into commercial performance, customer value, and operational performance. However, some areas require additional data before definitive conclusions can be made.
Delivery Delay Root Cause
The analysis identifies delivery reliability as a key operational risk but does not contain sufficient operational granularity to confirm the exact cause.
Further investigation requires:
•	Warehouse-level data 
•	Carrier performance data 
•	Inventory availability information 
•	Order processing timestamps 
Customer Behaviour Analysis
The current dataset identifies valuable customer groups but does not include historical behaviour.
Future analysis could include:
•	Customer retention rates 
•	Repeat purchase frequency 
•	Customer lifetime value 
•	Churn analysis 
•	Loyalty tier movement 
Supply Chain Risk Analysis
Average supplier lead time is consistent across categories, but averages may hide supplier-specific risks.
Future analysis should include:
•	Supplier OTIF performance 
•	Lead-time variability 
•	Supplier dependency risk 
•	Purchase order reliability
