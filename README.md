# Harbour & Co. Retail Operations Analytics

## Project Overview

**Project Name:** Harbour & Co. Retail Operations Analytics  
**Project Role:** Senior Business Analyst — Retail Operations & Omnichannel Transformation (Simulated)  
**Business Area:** Retail Operations, Supply Chain & Digital Transformation  
**Organisation:** Harbour & Co. Retail  

**Note:** All financial, operational, and customer figures used within this project are simulated assumptions created to represent a realistic premium retail organisation. The project is designed for portfolio demonstration purposes.

---

# 1. Executive Summary

Harbour & Co. Retail Operations Analytics is an end-to-end Business Analysis and data analytics project developed to support **Project Horizon** — Harbour & Co.'s Omnichannel Transformation Programme.

The project demonstrates how operational data can be transformed into actionable insights to support strategic decision-making across retail operations, supply chain, customer experience, and commercial performance.

The solution establishes a relational data foundation covering:

- Customer behaviour
- Sales performance
- Product performance
- Inventory operations
- Supplier management
- Procurement
- Order fulfilment
- Shipment performance

The objective of this engagement was to provide leadership with a data-driven understanding of commercial performance, customer value drivers, and operational constraints impacting sustainable omnichannel growth.

---

# 2. Company Overview

Harbour & Co. Retail is a premium UK omnichannel retailer specialising in:

- Fashion
- Home products

- Consumer products

The organisation has established itself as a trusted premium retail brand through a nationwide store network and strong customer relationships.

As consumer purchasing behaviour continues shifting towards digital channels, Harbour & Co. has accelerated investment into its e-commerce capabilities and omnichannel operating model.

This transformation has increased the need for stronger analytical capabilities to support decision-making across commercial operations, customer experience, fulfilment, and supply chain functions.

# Dashboard 1 — Executive Business Performance Overview

## Objective

Evaluate Harbour & Co.'s commercial health, channel performance, and profitability.

## Dashboard

![Executive Business Performance Overview](dashboard_1_executive_business_performance.png)

## Key Findings

- The analysed dataset represents **£282M revenue** and **£132M profit**.
- Website is the largest individual sales channel, generating approximately **£113M revenue and £53M profit**.
- Website, Click & Collect, and Marketplace collectively contribute approximately **70% of revenue and profit**.
- Profitability remains consistent across channels at approximately **46–48%**, indicating that digital growth is not currently reducing profitability.

## Strategic Implication

Harbour & Co. should continue investing in its omnichannel strategy while ensuring fulfilment capability scales alongside digital demand.

# Dashboard 2 — Customer & Product Value Analysis

## Objective

Identify the customer segments and product categories generating the greatest commercial value.

## Dashboard

![Customer and Product Value Analysis](dashboard_2_customer_product_value_analysis.png)

## Key Findings

### Customer Value

- Regular customers contribute approximately **£155M revenue and £73M profit**, representing the largest customer segment.
- Loyalty members contribute approximately **83% of dashboard revenue**.
- Silver loyalty members represent the largest individual loyalty group.

### Product Value

- Lifestyle and Fashion are the strongest commercial categories.
- Together they generate approximately **£191M revenue and £92M profit**.
- These categories represent the highest priority areas for protecting availability and customer experience.

## Strategic Implication

Harbour & Co. should prioritise customer retention strategies and protect operational performance within commercially important categories.

# Dashboard 3 — Operations & Supply Chain Performance Review

## Objective

Assess whether Harbour & Co.'s operational capability supports continued omnichannel growth.

## Dashboard

![Operations and Supply Chain Performance Review](dashboard_3_operations_supply_chain_performance.png)

## Key Findings

### Delivery Performance

- Approximately **70% of online orders are delivered on time**.
- Around **87K orders experienced delays** during the analysed period.
- Website represents approximately **80% of delayed orders**, making it the highest-impact area for improvement.

### Supplier Performance

- Average supplier lead time is approximately **33 days across categories**.
- Category-level variation is minimal.
- Supplier lead time is therefore not identified as the primary explanation for delivery delays.

## Strategic Implication

The immediate operational priority is improving fulfilment reliability through further investigation of warehouse operations, inventory availability, carrier performance, and fulfilment processes.

# 8. Executive Strategic Assessment

The analysis indicates that Harbour & Co. has a strong commercial foundation, with profitable omnichannel operations and clear areas of customer and product value concentration.

However, as the organisation continues its digital transformation journey, operational reliability has emerged as the key constraint to sustainable growth.

The strategic assessment is summarised across four themes:

---

## 1. Omnichannel Growth Remains a Strategic Advantage

Digital channels generate approximately 70% of revenue and profit within the analysed dataset, while maintaining consistent profitability.

**Business implication:**

Harbour & Co. should continue investing in digital growth while ensuring operational capability scales with customer demand.

---

## 2. Customer Value is Concentrated Among Existing Relationships

Regular customers and loyalty members represent the majority of commercial value.

**Business implication:**

Retention, personalisation, and loyalty progression should be prioritised to maximise customer lifetime value.

---

## 3. Core Categories Require Protection

Lifestyle and Fashion generate approximately 70% of profit.

**Business implication:**

Availability, assortment quality, and fulfilment performance should be prioritised within these categories.

---

## 4. Fulfilment Reliability is the Primary Operational Risk

Delivery delays represent the most significant operational challenge identified.

**Business implication:**

Harbour & Co. should focus on improving fulfilment reliability before accelerating further digital expansion.
